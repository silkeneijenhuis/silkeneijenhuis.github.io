Interactive prototype with new features for survey order form

Interactive prototype with new features for survey order form

Goals

PlaytestCloud offers insights from real players by recording them on their phone while they play the game under real conditions. Game developers can order these playtests through order forms on www.playtestcloud.com. For this project the goal was to optimize the ordering process for surveys, including new features as well as adding new options to existing features.

Design process

For this project I became a list of features with the task to create a prototype how the features could be implemented. First I created mockups with ideas for each feature and discussed them with the product owner as well as the person who collected the feature requests from the customers. After several instances I created the prototpye which can be used to show the upcoming features to the customers as well as the template for the implementation in the developerment department.

One of the requested features was to be able to re-order the questions within the survey. At first there was the need of a layout that uses less vertical space so more questions are visible on one screen fold. To use less vertical space I removed emty spaces and used icons instead of butons or checkboxes for question options. Also I changed the question type selection from a button group to a dropdown menu.
To make clear what will be moved when changing the order of quesitons I put each question in a separate box. When hovering over the sort icon on top of the box the defautl mouse curses changes to the grab curser. The questions can be moved by drag and drop.

I used the Sketch app to create the mockups for distribution.

I used the Craft by Invision Sketch plug-in to create an interactive prototype with the mockups.

Microphone check before video recording to improve audio quality

Details app

Goal

PlaytestCloud offers insights from real players by recording them on their phone while they play the game under real conditions. Often the audio qualitiy of videos wasn't sufficient. The goal for this project was to improve the qualitiy through out the videos.

Design process

First I reviewed videos which were reported as unsufficent audio quality. Most of the reasons were not propably working headsets, the microphone was too close to the mouth and something was rubbing againg the microphone. To make the players aware of the issue with their headset I created the microphone check as part of the onboarding process inside the app. The microphone check forces the player to record a short audio frequence and listen to it afterwards.

This feature was part of a re-design of the unboarding process.

Tools

#Pen and paper

I used pen and paper to sketch first ideas as a basis for further discussions with the stakeholders.

#Adobe Illustrator

I used Adobe Illustrator to create a detailed mockup for user testing and implementation.

#Marvel app

I created an interactive prototype in Marvel app with screens I have created with Adobe Illustrator.

GIF animation "Trust the developers"

Details gif

Goal

PlaytestCloud offers insights from real players by recording them on their phone while they play the game under real conditions. The players have to install a special PlaytestCloud version of the game on their phone. With iOS 9 Apple introduces the requirement to manually establish trust for manually installed apps. The goal for this project was to explain how the players trust the developers and are able to open the game.

Design process

Because at PlaytestCloud I also was responsible for customer support I was in contact with the players. As this issue came up when the players installed the latest iOS version on their phones and tablets in the beginning I explained the workaround to them personally. During this time I learned that the players need a visual explanation. Furthermore it was important to show the explaination on one screen of an iPhone otherwise the picuters of the explaination would be too small or not be read completely. So I created an animated GIF that shows all the steps in the settings menu in one picture. A GIF is also easy to implent and consumes less mobile data compared to a video.

Tools

#Adobe Premier

I used Adobe Premier to cut and edit the video I recorded with a screenrecorder.

#Adobe Photoshop

I used Adobe Photoshop to add touches where the players have to tap. Also I used Adobe Photoshop to export the video as a GIF-file.

Mockup for new video player to display playtest results to customers

Details videoplayer

Goals

PlaytestCloud offers insights from real players by recording them on their phone while they play the game under real conditions. After the playtest was completed the game developers can watch the videos, annotated them, read the survey results and share the insights with their team. For this project the gaol was to re-design the video player so all information, including the video, annotations and survey results, can be displayed on one screen fold. One importent point was that the video is always visible and no scrolling is needed. Furthermore days and sessions of multisession playtests and longitudial studies should be add to the video player.

Design process

In a brainstorming session with the product owner, the head of customer relations and me we collected all information we need to display in the video player. We also discuessed the layout how the days and sessions of multisession playtests and longitudinal studies should be shown. The collected information and ideas were documented by handdrawn sketches by me (as shown in the picture).

In the next step I created a detailed mockup as a template for the implentation through the development department.

Tools

#Pen and paper/whiteboard

I used pen and paper and the whiteboard to collect all the information and ideas from the brainstorming session. It was also used as a basis for further discussions on each feature.

#Adobe Illustrator

I used Adobe Illustrator to create the detailed mockups.
